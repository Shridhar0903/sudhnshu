About Carbon Steel

Carbon steel is considered to be carbon steel when no minimum content is specified or required for chromium, cobalt, molybdenum, nickel, niobium, titanium, tungsten, vanadium or zirconium, or any other element to be added to obtain a desired alloying effect.

The term “carbon steel” may also be used in reference to steel which is not stainless steel. In this use, carbon steel may include alloy steels.

As the carbon content rises, steel has the ability to become harder and stronger through heat treatment, but this also makes it less ductile. Regardless of the heat treatment, higher carbon content reduces weld ability. In carbon steels, the higher carbon content lowers the melting point.

Applications

Pure Tungsten (Green): Best suited for AC TIG welding of aluminium and magnesium alloys.

Thoriated (Red / Yellow / Orange): Best suited for DC TIG welding of carbon steel, stainless steel, nickel alloys and titanium.

Lanthanated (Black / Gold / Blue): Suitable for AC & DC welding applications with good arc characteristics.

Ceriated (Grey / Orange): Best suited for low amperage DC welding with stable arc start.

Zirconiated (White / Brown): Best suited for AC TIG welding with higher contamination resistance.

Technical Notes

W / WP / Green: Non-radioactive; suitable for AC welding of aluminium, magnesium and their alloys.

WT10 / WT20: Excellent electron emission and high current carrying capacity; suitable for DC welding.

WL10 / WL15 / WL20: Non-radioactive; excellent conductivity and welding capacity.

WC10 / WC15 / WC20: Easier arc initiation under low current circumstances.

WZ8: Suitable for AC welding and preferred for radiographic quality welding.
